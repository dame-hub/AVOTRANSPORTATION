Two Engines: Fleet Contracts & a Food Brand.

How AVO's marketing arm drives B2B fleet detailing contracts (sales-led) and builds the AVO Kitchen food brand on social media (audience-led) — two different playbooks, one team, one budget.

Part A — Marketing Fleet Detailing (B2B)

Fleet contracts are not won by going viral — they're won by getting in front of a fleet manager with proof and a no-risk offer. The engine is targeted outreach + credibility, repeated.

Positioning

The one-line promise "AVO keeps your fleet clean and on-brand — detailed at your yard, on a schedule, at one flat per-vehicle rate we'll beat any written quote on."

Ideal Customer Profile (who to spend on)

  • 20–80 vehicle fleets that park overnight in one yard in the Denver metro.
  • Brand-visible vehicles (wrapped vans, delivery, rental, dealership lots) where clean = revenue.
  • One decision-maker (owner / fleet / ops manager) who can sign.

Part B — Marketing AVO Kitchen on Social.

The food brand is won the opposite way: short-form video builds a hungry local audience, then Facebook Marketplace pre-orders, a recipe shop, and pop-ups convert it to cash.

Compliance guardrail (don't skip) Do not advertise hot lamb chop / oxtail plates or lemonade for sale until the Retail Food License + commissary are live (Food Brand Plan, Phase 2). In Phase 1, market the brand, the cook, recipes, and seasoning — build the audience legally, then flip the switch on plate pre-orders.

Platform strategy

PlatformJob
TikTokTop of funnel — viral sizzle/ASMR cooking videos to reach new Denver locals fast.
Instagram ReelsSame content; brand home, story polls, "drop day" countdowns.
Facebook (Page + Marketplace + groups)Where the money is — pre-order listings, local food groups, older buyers with cash to spend.
YouTube ShortsRecycle the best clips; evergreen recipe discovery.

Content pillars (what to post)

1. The Sizzle

Close-up ASMR of lamb chops hitting the grill, oxtails falling off the bone, lemonade pour. 7–20s, trending audio, strong first-frame hook. This is the reach driver.

2. The Cook / The Brand

You on camera — story, neighborhood, "why oxtail." Faces build trust and following far faster than food alone.

3. Recipe Teasers → Sell

"Here's how I season my chops — full recipe at the link." Drives recipe-card / e-book sales (legal in Phase 1).

4. Proof & Drops

Customer plates, sold-out drops, "this week's menu," countdowns. Creates urgency and social proof (Phase 2 for plates).

The content → cash funnel

  1. Short-form video (TikTok/Reels) gets reach with a scroll-stopping food hook.
  2. Viewers hit the profile → one clear link (Stan/Beacons): recipes + seasoning now, plate pre-orders once licensed.
  3. Facebook Marketplace + groups carry the weekly pre-order menu; deposits lock orders.
  4. Email/DM list from buyers → blast every new drop. Owned audience, no algorithm risk.
  5. Pop-ups & catering upsell the superfans; co-host at AVO detailing events / car meets.

Cadence, hooks & tactics

  • Post 1–2 short videos/day across TikTok + Reels; volume + consistency beats perfection.
  • Lead every video with a hook in the first 1 second (the sizzle, "POV: best oxtail in Denver").
  • Use local + food hashtags/geo-tags: #DenverFood #AuroraCO #oxtail #lambchops #DenverEats.
  • Go live while cooking on drop days; live viewers convert to pre-orders.
  • Reply to every comment fast (early engagement = more reach); pin "how to order."
  • Re-list Marketplace posts weekly; the algorithm favors fresh listings.
  • Cross-promote with AVO's detailing audience — same orange brand, shared local trust.
