Who To Target & Where The Fleets Are.

A prioritized, Denver-metro-wide research brief: the exact business types with vehicle fleets in your service area, named companies and where their yards sit, who to ask for, and how to turn each into a 20–80 vehicle detailing contract.

Your base & radius Base: Aurora, CO. Coverage for this brief: full Denver metro — Aurora, Denver, Commerce City, Lakewood, Arvada, Westminster, Thornton, Centennial, Littleton, Englewood, Golden, plus the DIA / Gateway / Montbello industrial logistics belt where most large fleets actually park overnight.

Where Fleets Physically Park (drive these first)

Fleets are easiest to win when you detail them where they sleep — overnight in a yard, all vehicles in one place, no scheduling around routes. The metro's dense fleet-yard zones:

  • Gateway / DIA cargo & logistics belt (NE Aurora & NE Denver) — last-mile delivery stations, freight, rental-car ready lots, shuttle yards. Highest fleet density in the metro and closest to your Aurora base.
  • Commerce City / I-70 / Vasquez corridor — construction, trades, freight, municipal yards, fleet-maintenance shops.
  • Montbello / Central Park (Stapleton) NE Denver — service trades, delivery, contractor fleets.
  • S. Havana St "auto row," Aurora — dealership lots packed with inventory (see Dealerships below).
  • Englewood / Sheridan & N. Washington (Thornton/Northglenn) — HVAC/plumbing/electrical trade fleets and equipment yards.

How To Build Your Live Target List (do this in a week)

  1. Google Maps grid sweep — search "delivery service," "HVAC," "plumber," "landscaping," "car rental," "used cars," "construction" across each fleet-yard zone above. Drop every one with a yard of vehicles into a spreadsheet: name, address, est. fleet size, phone, website.
  2. Amazon DSP directory — logistics.amazon.com and DSP listing sites name the stations serving Denver/Aurora; each is a 20–60 van target.
  3. Drive the yards at 6–7am — count vehicles parked overnight; that's your real fleet-size estimate and proof you can service them before routes leave.
  4. County / city business-license rolls & LinkedIn — find the "Fleet Manager" / "Operations Manager" name before you call; ask for them by name.
  5. Score & rank — columns for fleet size (20–80 sweet spot), overnight-yard (yes = easy), brand-visibility (wrapped = motivated), and distance from Aurora. Sort; work top-down.
