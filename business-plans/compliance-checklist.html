Stay Legal & Insured — Both Businesses.

A working compliance checklist for AVO's two ventures in Colorado: the food brand (AVO Kitchen) and the detailing / fleet operation. Tick each item; verify current fees and rules with the listed agency. This is guidance, not legal advice.

How to use Items marked [NOW] apply before you take your first dollar. [PLATES] apply only once you sell hot food. [HIRE] apply once you have employees. When in doubt, call the agency named — a 10-minute call beats a fine or shutdown.

Shared — Both Businesses

  • [NOW] LLC in good standing — AVO Transportation LLC active with the Colorado Secretary of State; file the annual Periodic Report on time.
  • [NOW] Trade names (DBAs) — register "AVO Kitchen" (and any detailing brand) as trade names under the LLC with the CO SOS.
  • [NOW] EIN — federal Employer Identification Number from the IRS for the LLC.
  • [NOW] Business bank account — keep personal and business funds separate (protects the LLC liability shield); sub-track food vs. detailing.
  • [NOW] Sales-tax licenses — Colorado Dept. of Revenue state license, plus Aurora (home-rule city) and any other home-rule city you sell in (Denver, etc.) collect their own — register with each.
  • [NOW] General liability insurance — confirm coverage and limits; tell your agent about every activity you do.
  • [NOW] Recordkeeping — keep income/expense records and tax filings (income, sales/use, self-employment).
  • [HIRE] Workers' comp + payroll — Colorado requires workers' compensation for any employees; set up payroll-tax withholding, file I-9s, and unemployment insurance.

Food Business — AVO Kitchen (Colorado)

Phase 1 — Cottage / digital (legal to start today)

  • [NOW] Food-safety training — complete a cottage-food safety course (CSU Extension) or a Food Handler card before selling any food product. Keep the certificate (good ~3 years).
  • [NOW] Sell only cottage-eligible items — dry spice/seasoning blends, candies, certain non-refrigerated baked goods, honey, jams. No meat. No beverages/lemonade. No refrigerated foods.
  • [NOW] Cottage labeling — each product labeled with producer name & address (or registration), product name, ingredients, net weight, date, and the required statement that it was "produced in a home kitchen that is not licensed or inspected."
  • [NOW] Direct-to-consumer only — sell straight to the end customer; no sales to stores or restaurants for resale.
  • [NOW] $10,000 cap per product/year — track net sales per product variety; stay under the cottage limit.
  • [NOW] Recipes are not food — selling digital recipe cards/e-books needs no food license at all.
The hard line Lamb chops, oxtails, and lemonade cannot be sold under cottage law — full stop. Selling them from a home kitchen risks fines and a cease order. They require the Phase 2 license below. (Note: Colorado HB26-1033 expands cottage foods effective Jan 2027 — verify its final scope; it is unlikely to permit hot ready-to-eat meals.)

Phase 2 — Hot plates & lemonade (Retail Food License)

  • [PLATES] Retail Food Establishment license — apply through the county health authority where you operate: Adams County Health Department, Tri-County area / CDPHE for Arapahoe, or the relevant county; mobile & temporary-event vendors are licensed annually (expire Dec 31).
  • [PLATES] Commissary / commercial kitchen agreement — a signed agreement with a licensed, inspected kitchen for prep, storage & warewashing (required for mobile/caterer applications; commissary often must be in the same county).
  • [PLATES] Certified Food Protection Manager — at least one ServSafe Manager (or equivalent) certificate for the operation.
  • [PLATES] Plan review & inspection — pass the health department's plan review and pre-operational inspection; keep a probe thermometer (0–220°F) and temperature logs.
  • [PLATES] Mobile / temporary event permits — separate permits per event/venue; vehicle lettering & identification per county rules if mobile.
  • [PLATES] Product-liability / food insurance — add a food rider; your detailing GL will not cover food-borne illness.
  • [PLATES] Prepared-food sales tax — collect & remit; prepared food is taxed differently than groceries and than services.
  • [PLATES] Fire / propane permits — if cooking with propane at events, comply with the local fire authority.
  • [PLATES] Allergen & labeling — disclose major allergens; date and label packaged items.

Detailing & Fleet — Compliance.

The big one for mobile detailing is wash-water — get that right and the rest is standard small-business licensing and insurance.

#1 risk for mobile detailing — wash-water runoff Under the federal Clean Water Act and local stormwater (MS4) ordinances in Aurora, Denver & metro cities, soapy/dirty wash water may NOT enter storm drains, gutters, or streets. You must capture/reclaim wash water (mats, berms, vacuum recovery) and dispose of it to a sanitary sewer or approved site, or wash where runoff is contained. Violations carry real fines. Build reclamation into every on-site fleet job and put it in your client agreements.

Detailing Business — Colorado

Licensing & tax

  • [NOW] City business license — register/obtain a business license with the City of Aurora (and any other city where you base operations); confirm requirements for mobile service.
  • [NOW] Sales/use tax — Colorado labor-only services are generally not taxed, but tangible materials/products (e.g., ceramic coatings, retail products) and some bundled services can be taxable; home-rule cities (Aurora, Denver) set their own rules — register and confirm with each.
  • [NOW] Use-tax on supplies/equipment — pay use tax on equipment/chemicals bought tax-free or out of state, where applicable.

Environmental & safety

  • [NOW] Wash-water reclamation — equipment & procedure to capture and lawfully dispose of runoff (see warning above); no discharge to storm drains.
  • [NOW] Chemical handling & SDS — keep Safety Data Sheets for all products; store, label & dispose per manufacturer and local rules; OSHA hazard-communication if you have staff.
  • [NOW] Waste disposal — dispose of contaminated water, filters & rags properly; no phosphate-laden runoff to waterways.
  • [NOW] Water source — if drawing from a client/hydrant, do it lawfully and with backflow prevention where required.

Insurance & contracts

  • [NOW] Commercial General Liability — at least $1M/occurrence; the baseline clients will ask for.
  • [NOW] Garage Keepers / care-custody-and-control — covers damage to client vehicles in your care; essential for fleet work and usually required by fleet clients.
  • [NOW] Commercial auto — for AVO's own service vehicles/trailers.
  • [NOW] Certificates of Insurance (COIs) — be ready to issue COIs and name fleet clients as additional insured (your contract already allows this).
  • [NOW] Inland marine / equipment — optional cover for your pressure washers, extractors & tools.

Employees / crew

  • [HIRE] Workers' compensation — mandatory in Colorado for employees; verify status of any 1099 contractors (misclassification is penalized).
  • [HIRE] Payroll & I-9 / E-Verify — withholding, unemployment insurance, work-authorization verification.
  • [HIRE] Driver checks & training — MVR checks for anyone driving client vehicles; train crew on wash-water & chemical procedures.

Fleet-contract specifics

  • Signed Fleet Detailing Services Agreement for every account (see the contract document) — scope, tiers, pricing, insurance, liability, Colorado venue.
  • Pre-existing damage documentation — photograph notable damage before service (protects you under the contract's liability terms).
  • Vendor onboarding — larger/municipal clients may require W-9, COIs, background checks & their own vendor agreement; keep these on file.
Quick-start order of operations 1) LLC good-standing + DBAs → 2) EIN + bank + sales-tax licenses → 3) insurance (GL + garage keepers) → 4) wash-water reclamation set up → 5) food: safety cert + cottage items now, Retail Food License before any plates. Do these and both businesses are operating on solid legal footing.

Agencies to verify with: Colorado Secretary of State; IRS; Colorado Dept. of Revenue; City of Aurora (and Denver/other home-rule cities); CDPHE Division of Environmental Health & Sustainability; Adams County Health Department / county health authority; CSU Extension (cottage training); EPA & municipal stormwater (MS4) programs; Colorado Dept. of Labor & Employment (workers' comp). Rules and fees change — confirm current requirements before relying on this list.
