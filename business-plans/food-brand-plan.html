The Hot Plate Brand Plan.

A bulletproof, phased plan to build a Denver food brand around fresh, hot, ready-to-eat plates — lamb chops, oxtails, and lemonade — sold through social media, Facebook Marketplace, and recipe drops, operated as a division of AVO Transportation LLC.

Read this first — the one thing that will sink the plan if ignored Under Colorado's Cottage Foods Act (the home-kitchen law), you cannot legally sell lamb chops, oxtails, or lemonade from your home kitchen. Cottage law only covers non-potentially-hazardous, shelf-stable foods. Meat dishes are "potentially hazardous" (temperature-controlled), and beverages/juices are specifically excluded. Selling hot meat plates requires a licensed kitchen + a Retail Food License. The plan below is built so you can start building the brand and earning today — legally — and switch on hot-plate sales the moment you have the license. Don't post "DM to order plates" until Phase 2 is live.

The Brand in One Line

AVO Kitchen — "Denver's slow-cooked oxtail & flame-grilled lamb chop plates, plus the recipes to make them yourself." Same trusted AVO name and orange mark customers already see on the trucks. The food brand rides the goodwill, audience, and local reach AVO already has.

Two-Track Strategy (start earning now, unlock plates next)

Track A — legal today

Brand, audience & cash-flow with zero food license

  • Digital recipes — your oxtail & lamb chop recipes as PDF cards / a mini e-book. Selling information is not food and needs no license at all.
  • Branded dry seasoning & rubs — "AVO Oxtail Seasoning," "Lamb Chop Rub," jerk/Cajun blends. Dry spice blends are cottage-legal in Colorado.
  • Cottage-legal treats — candies, certain baked goods, dehydrated items to round out the shop.
  • The content engine — film every cook now; build the following before you can even sell a plate.
Track B — the real money

Hot, fresh, ready plates (needs a license)

  • Lamb chop plates, oxtail plates, sides, fresh lemonade.
  • Sold as pre-order pickup, pop-ups, and events — not random home sales.
  • Requires a commissary/commercial kitchen + Retail Food License (mobile or temporary-event). See Phase 2.
  • This is where margins and the "brand" really come alive — Track A exists to make Track B launch to a warm crowd.

Where You Sell & How Money Comes In

  • Facebook Marketplace — list weekly "pre-order pickup" drops (Phase 2) and your seasoning + recipe products (Phase 1). Renew/refresh listings often; Marketplace rewards fresh posts.
  • Pre-order pickup model — post the week's menu, take orders + deposits by Wed, cook a known quantity, customers pick up at a set time/place. Near-zero waste, predictable buys, no street-vending guesswork.
  • Local Facebook groups — Denver/Aurora foodie, "what's for dinner," neighborhood, and church/community groups (follow each group's selling rules).
  • Recipe shop — a free Stan Store / Gumroad / Beacons link in every bio to sell recipes & seasoning 24/7.
  • Pop-ups & events (Phase 2+) — barbershops, car meets (tie-in with AVO detailing events), markets, festivals with a temporary event permit.

Startup Costs & Break-Even

Phase 1 (legal today)

  • Food-safety handler course — $10–25
  • Trade name (DBA) with CO SOS — ~$25
  • Seasoning jars, labels, ingredients — ~$150–300
  • Recipe design (Canva, free) — $0
  • Phone tripod / light for filming — ~$40

Start for under ~$400.

Phase 2 (hot plates)

  • Commissary kitchen — $99–$675/mo
  • Retail Food License — ~$100–400/yr (county)
  • Sales-tax licenses (state + Aurora) — ~$20–50
  • Food manager cert (ServSafe) — ~$150
  • Liability insurance — ~$40–70/mo
  • Packaging, first inventory — ~$300–500

~$1,000–1,800 to go live, then ~$300–800/mo fixed.

The math that makes it bulletproof At a ~$22 lamb chop plate with ~$11 food cost, you net roughly $11 per plate. Covering a $99/mo Colorado Kitchen Share commissary + ~$60/mo insurance (~$160 fixed) takes only ~15 plates a month. Everything past that is profit. Pre-orders mean you never cook a plate you haven't already sold — the model is built to not lose money.

Operating Under AVO Transportation LLC

  • File a trade name (DBA) "AVO Kitchen" under the existing LLC with the CO Secretary of State (~$25) — one entity, one tax return.
  • Tell your insurer you're adding food operations; add a product-liability / food rider (your current detailing GL will not cover food-borne illness claims).
  • Keep separate books (own bank sub-account, own expense tracking) so food P&L is clean even though taxes flow through the one LLC.
  • Use a separate sales-tax line — prepared food is taxed differently than detailing services in Colorado/Aurora.
